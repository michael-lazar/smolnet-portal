You can upload a self-signed TLS client certificate in order to make authenticated requests to gemini:// servers. Your certificate and private key will be stored on the portal server for the lifetime of your session, and will only be sent to hosts that you choose to activate them for.

This feature is intended for testing and other low-stakes use. Your certificate will be stored unencrypted on the server. I will not use, copy, or share it under any circumstances.

Don't have a certificate? You can generate one with openssl 
  openssl req -x509 -newkey rsa:2048 -sha256 -days 36500 -nodes \
      -keyout portal-user.key -out portal-user.crt -subj "/CN=portal-user"
